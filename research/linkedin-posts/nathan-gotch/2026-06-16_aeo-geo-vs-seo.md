## Post Summary

Nathan examines whether **AEO/GEO (AI search optimization)** is just traditional SEO.

---

## Core Insights

### 1. Perplexity ≈ Google
- Strong correlation between Google rankings and Perplexity citations  
- In many cases: **near 100% alignment**

👉 Implication:
- Ranking well in Google often → visibility in Perplexity

---

### 2. ChatGPT ≠ Google
- ChatGPT uses **different sources and retrieval methods**
- Does NOT heavily rely on Google’s index

👉 Implication:
- Traditional SEO alone is **insufficient** for ChatGPT visibility

---

### 3. AI Search Is Fragmented
Each platform behaves differently:

| Platform    | Behavior |
|------------|---------|
| Google     | Traditional ranking |
| Perplexity | Mirrors Google heavily |
| ChatGPT    | Diverse, non-Google sources |
| Brave      | Independent data inputs |

---

## Key Takeaways

- SEO still matters — especially for some AI engines  
- But **platform-specific optimization is required**  
- AI search = **multi-engine ecosystem**, not one system  

---

## Strategic Conclusion

> "If you rank in Google, you're likely to show in some AI systems — but not all."

Modern strategy:
- Combine **SEO + AEO + brand/entity building**
